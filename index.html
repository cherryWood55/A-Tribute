Lata Mangeshkar

Queen of Melody

Here's a timeline of Mangeshkar's life:

  • 1929 - Born on 28 September, 1929 in Indore, MP to Pandit Deenanath Mangeshkar and Shevanti
  • 1942 - Began her career as an actor and singer after the demise of her father and sang her first song for Marathi movie Kiti Hasaal. The song was dropped from the final cut of the movie.
  • 1943 - Her first Hindi song was Mata Ek Sapoot Ki Duniya Badal De Tu for the movie Gajaabhaau (Marathi)
  • 1945 - Started classical music training from Ustad Aman Ali Khan
  • 1948 - Got first major break with the song Dil Mera Toda from the movie Majboor
  • 1949 - First major hit was Aayega Aanewaala from the movie Mahal
  • 1950 - Collaborated with successful composers like SD Burman, Naushad Ali, Salil Chowdhury and Madan Mohan and sang for movies like Shree 420 (1955) and Devdas (1955)
  • 1955 - Composed music for the first time for Ram Ram Pavhane (Marathi movie)
  • 1960 - Composed music for several Marathi movies under the pseudonym of Anand Ghan
  • 1969 - Awarded the Padma Bhushan
  • 1973 - Awarded the National Award for the Best Female Playback Singer for the movie Parichay
  • 1974 - Became the first Indian artist to perform at the Royal Albert Hall
  • 1975 - Featured in Guiness Book of World Records for having made the most recordings in the world; the listing later ran into controversy
  • 1989 - Founded Lata Mangeshkar Medical Foundation, along with her siblings
  • 1990 - Launched her own production house and produced Gulzar's movie Lekin...
  • 1991 - Awarded the Padma Vibhushan Award
  • 2000 - Established Master Deenanath Mangeshkar Hospital, in Pune
  • 2001 - Awarded the Bharat Ratna
  • 2004 - Two songs, Tere Sang Pyar Mein, and Wada Na Tod, sung by her appeared in the movie Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind
  • 2007 - Awarded the Legion of Honor
  • 2012 - launched her own music label called LM Music
  • 2022 - Passed away on 6th February, 2022 at Breach Candy Hospital Trust, Mumbai

I wouldn't let a biopic be made on me. That's because I don't believe in them. I don't like them.

-- Lata Mangeshkar
